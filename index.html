📣 Hey, Open Source enthusiasts!

To all our budding developers, particularly our interns and juniors: When you push your projects to GitHub, it's not just about the code. It's about your journey and vision too.

1️⃣ Why did you start this project? Your inspiration could ignite a spark in someone else. Share the purpose and the end goal.

2️⃣ What should your project achieve? Detail the features and functionalities you envision. It gives a clearer picture to anyone looking.

3️⃣ Feeling stuck somewhere? Don’t just abandon it. Mention where you’re struggling. Someone out there might have just the solution or a fresh perspective on it.

By being transparent and communicating your intent, you make it easier for the next developer who stumbles upon your project. They could decide if they have the expertise or interest to contribute and take it forward. Let's build a community that values collective growth and continuity. After all, why start from scratch when we can build upon each other's foundations?

Use the hashtags #open-to-collaborate or #open-to-contribute to signal your interest and willingness to work with others.

Let’s respect and value each other's time and effort. Together, we grow! 🌱🔧🌍

P.S. Even small README.md on what you struggling or something about missing tool, could help others to join and help you.
